How to Use

Indicator Logic

  • Corr. (Correlation to BTC): 0.0–1.0 range. Closer to 1 → moves in sync with BTC. Closer to 0 → moves independently.
  • EMA 200 (1h):
    • Green — price above EMA200 (bullish bias).
    • Red — price below EMA200 (bearish bias).
    • Gray — price near EMA200 (neutral).
  • RSI (1h & 15m):
    • Green — oversold (potential bounce).
    • Red — overbought (possible pullback).
    • Gray — neutral momentum.
  • MACD 15m:
    • Green — MACD above signal line & above zero (bullish).
    • Red — MACD below signal line (bearish).
    • Gray — no clear signal.
  • Relative Volume: Compares current volume to last 20 candles’ average. Above 1 → higher-than-average activity.

Strategies

1. Trend Following

  • Context: EMA200 green, RSI neutral/green, MACD green.
  • Entry: Buy pullbacks with volume confirmation.
  • Exit: First resistance or RSI 15m overbought.

2. Reversal

  • Context: EMA200 red, RSI 1h green, MACD turning green.
  • Entry: Avoid high BTC correlation (Corr. < 0.5).
  • Exit: EMA200 retest or key level.

3. Breakout with Volume

  • Context: EMA200 neutral, RSI 15m green, volume > 2× avg, MACD green.
  • Entry: Breakout above consolidation with high volume.
  • Exit: Trailing stop below last candle lows.

4. BTC-Correlated Momentum

  • Context: Corr. > 0.85, BTC trending strongly.
  • Entry: Same direction as BTC after confirmation.
  • Exit: BTC momentum fades or RSI 15m turns red.
